Here you can find population data for tigers in the Sunderbans from 2007 to 2012.Starting in 2007, a tiger monitoring system was implemented in sunderban forest.Data from 4 ranges were collected each year and data consisted of tigers track set around the river banks.A tiger moves on average 1.65-1.72 km/day.Distances moved include traversing both terrestrial habitat and waterways,a tiger crosses a river banks 10-15 times/month (equivalent to one crossing every two to three days). This frequent rate of water way crossing suggests that a monitoring survey, based on track counts along creek banks, would have a high chance of detecting tiger presence.

Tiger Poaching

Tiger poaching is driven in part by the traditional Asian medicine trade and is documented as the most important threat to tiger populations in nearly all the landscapes where they occur.

Loss and degradation of tiger habitat

Loss and or degradation of tiger habitat could also lead to a drop in the tiger population. However, due to the long-term nature of these habitat threats (e.g. sea level rise, overexploitation of forest resources, invasive species, encroachment) it is unlikely to create such a large decline in relative tiger abundance in such a short (5 year) time span.

Stray tiger killing

During 2008 and 2012, 5 stray tigers were killed in the Sundarbans East Division community area and another 6 stray tigers were killed in the West Division community area. These are just the official reported numbers.This can be one of the factors.
